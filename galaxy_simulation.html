Celestial Body Simulation

Our project represents a sophisticated endeavor in the realm of astrophysics â€“ a meticulous physical simulation capturing the intricate movements of celestial bodies.

This simulation comprehensively models the gravitational forces of mutual attraction between these celestial entities, incorporating factors such as mass and various physical properties.

In this simulation, the profound impact of celestial bodies colliding and the temporal distortions in accordance with Einstein's theories are faithfully replicated.

Moreover, our simulation offers the capability to manipulate time, allowing for accelerated progression and predictive analysis of future celestial phenomena.

A standout feature of our simulation is its flexibility. Users possess the ability to introduce new celestial bodies into the system, each with individually defined physical properties.

Witnessing the resultant interactions, and understanding how these new entities influence the pre-existing celestial bodies, adds an invaluable dimension to the study of celestial mechanics.

In essence, our simulation serves as an advanced tool for astronomers, physicists, and researchers, enabling a deeper understanding of the cosmos through precise, realistic modeling of celestial dynamics.
